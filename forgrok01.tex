\chapter{Introduction}

\groknote{Please read Appendix B first for instructions on how to read this document and your role in its continuing composition.}

This monograph is intended primarily as an articulation of a foundational account of an epistemologically rooted philosophical system.
It is however, practically motivated and so it may be helpful for me to begin with a brief account of those motivations.

First an account of the context in which this philosophical thinking takes place.
Philosophy is, for me, about the big picture, and I write it at a time of considerable significance for that big picture.

I think of myself as playing into the evolution and proliferation of \emph{intelligence}, originating in planet earth and spreading across the Milky Way galaxy and into the great Cosmos beyond.
I do not suppose that we are the only point of origin of intelligence, and I hope the ideas I present are compatible with there being many others, but this monograph is primarily written for \emph{homo sapiens}.
